What Is a Department Health Score

A department health score is a composite metric that aggregates multiple signals — engagement levels, sentiment trends, workload sustainability, leadership trust, and collaboration quality — into a single 0-100 score for each team or department. It's designed to answer the question every executive asks: "How are my teams doing?" with data, not speculation.

The Components

Engagement (30% weight)

Employee energy, purpose, and commitment to their work and team. Measured through sentiment analysis of AI conversation data.

Wellbeing (25% weight)

Workload sustainability, stress indicators, and work-life balance signals. Weighted heavily because burnout is the fastest path to attrition.

Leadership Trust (20% weight)

Confidence in direct management and executive leadership. A leading indicator of team stability and performance.

Collaboration (15% weight)

Cross-functional communication quality, information flow, and teamwork dynamics.

Growth (10% weight)

Career development satisfaction, learning opportunities, and sense of professional progress.

Acting on Scores

The value of department health scores isn't the number itself — it's the conversation it enables. When a department drops from 82 to 71 over two weeks, leaders can drill into the component scores to understand whether the decline is driven by workload issues, leadership changes, or cross-team friction.
